Poha

Once the best breakfast of our mess.

Gradually declined to now become just eatable.Nothing more,nothing less.

But you can count on it to give a filling meal to start your week.

Pro tip:Add the pre-cut tomatoes and onions to enhance flavour and texture.

Ingredients

  • 2.5 cup Poha (medium or thick)
  • 2 tablespoon Oil divided
  • 10-12 Curry Leaves
  • 2 Green Chili Pepper (diced)
  • ¾ cup Onions (diced)
  • ¾ cup Potato (diced into ½-1 cm pieces)
  • ¼ teaspoon Haldi
  • ½ teaspoon Salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon Sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon Lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon Corainder (chopped)

Instructions

  1. Add poha to a strainer, and rinse under running water until the water runs clear. Make sure all the poha is wet. Let it rest while you sauté the other ingredients.
  2. Heat a heavy bottom pan on medium-high heat. Add oil to the pan and let it heat.
  3. Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds to the oil, and let them sizzle.
  4. Add the curry leaves and green chili pepper. Then add the diced onions and stir well. Saute for a minute.
  5. Add the diced potatoes. Mix well with the onions. Now cover the pan with a lid and cook on medium-low heat until the potatoes are cooked completely. Open lid and stir. Break a potato piece with the spatula to make sure it is cooked well. (It is important to cut the potatoes to small pieces so they cook quickly in 3-4 minutes)
  6. Add turmeric, salt, sugar and poha to the pan. Mix gently, until all the poha gets the yellow turmeric color. Change to low heat and cover the pan for 2 minutes. This helps to heat the poha.
  7. Turn off heat and open the lid. Sprinkle lime juice.
  8. You can taste test, and adjust salt as needed. Garnish with cilantro leaves and any optional garnishes.
